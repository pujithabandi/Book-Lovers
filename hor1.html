The Shattered Reflection

Dr. Emma Taylor, a renowned psychologist, had always been fascinated by the human mind's darkest corners. Her latest project, the "Mirror Therapy" program, aimed to cure patients' deepest phobias by confronting their reflections. Within the abandoned Wingate Asylum, Emma established her laboratory, surrounded by shattered mirrors and the eerie silence of the abandoned wards. Her first patient, Tim, suffered from an intense fear of his own reflection. Emma's innovative technique involved immersing Tim in a mirrored room, forcing him to confront his fears. But something was amiss. During sessions, Tim's reflection began to distort, twisting into grotesque, inhuman visages. Emma dismissed it as a manifestation of Tim's subconscious. One fateful night, Emma entered the mirrored room to find Tim's reflection shattered, fragments of glass scattered everywhere. As she cleaned the shards, Emma noticed her own reflection warped, as if someone stood behind her. Suddenly, the mirrors exploded, unleashing a torrent of shattered glass and malevolent energy. Emma fled, but the reflections pursued her: - Twisted versions of herself and Tim stalked her through the asylum's corridors. - Mirrors seemed to breed new, monstrous reflections. - The air filled with whispers: "You'll never escape your own reflection." Emma discovered the dark history of Asylum: - A former patient, Malcolm, had been obsessed with mirrors, believing they held the key to other dimensions. - Malcolm's experiments unleashed an ancient entity, binding it to the mirrors. Now, Emma was trapped within the shattered glass labyrinth. As reflections closed in, Emma realized: - The entity fed on fear, growing stronger with each shattered mirror. - She had unleashed hell upon herself. In a desperate bid to escape, Emma smashed every remaining mirror, but the entity had already infected her mind. The last mirror shattered, revealing Emma's twisted reflection, forever trapped within the abyss of her own psyche. The asylum's darkness consumed her.
